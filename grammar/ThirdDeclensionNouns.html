Third Declension Nouns

The third declension in New Testament Greek encompasses a wide variety of noun forms and is characterized by its use of consonantal stems (as opposed to the vowel stems of the first and second declensions). These stems undergo irregular changes when combined with case endings, often resulting in contractions, elisions, or consonant assimilation. Because of this complexity, third declension nouns must be memorized with both their nominative and genitive singular forms to determine the stem.

Key Paradigms

πιστις, ἡ ("faith") – Consonantal Stem: πιστ-

Case Singular Plural
Nominative πίστις πίστεις
Genitive πίστεως πίστεων
Dative πίστει πίστεσι(ν)
Accusative πίστιν πίστεις

βασιλεύς, ὁ ("king") – Consonantal Stem: βασιλε-

Case Singular Plural
Nominative βασιλεύς βασιλεῖς
Genitive βασιλέως βασιλέων
Dative βασιλεῖ βασιλεῦσι(ν)
Accusative βασιλέα βασιλεῖς

σῶμα, τό ("body") – Neuter Stem: σωματ-

Case Singular Plural
Nominative σῶμα σώματα
Genitive σώματος σωμάτων
Dative σώματι σώμασι(ν)
Accusative σῶμα σώματα

Conceptual Categories

  • Consonantal Stems: Third declension nouns are built on consonantal stems (e.g., πιστ-, σωματ-, βασιλεϝ-). These include:
    • Dental stems (e.g., πίστις) that lose the dental (τ/δ/θ) before certain endings
    • Nasal stems (e.g., stems ending in -ν)
    • Labial stems and others, which may undergo assimilation or drop segments entirely
  • Case Endings and Irregularities: Third declension case endings differ significantly from first and second declensions. Notable features include:
    • Genitive singular ending: -ος or -εως (depending on the subclass)
    • Dative plural ending: -σι(ν), often appended directly to the stem
    • Accusative and nominative plural forms often identical in consonant and neuter stems
  • Accent Shifts and Stem Mutations: Because the stems vary in length and form, accentuation may shift between cases. For example:
    • In πίστις, the accent moves due to the increased syllabic length in genitive πίστεως
    • Neuter nouns like σῶμα maintain nominative and accusative identity and accent on the penult due to contraction
    • Stems such as βασιλεϝ- demonstrate vowel contraction and loss of digamma (ϝ) in the nominative
