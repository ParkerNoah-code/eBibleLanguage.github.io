Future Indicative Verbs

The future indicative in New Testament Greek expresses actions that are expected to occur in the future. While its primary function is to denote future time, the aspectual force is generally undefined or neutral. The future forms are closely related to the present indicative, but they are marked by characteristic morphological features, especially the use of the future tense stem, typically formed with the insertion of the σ marker.

Key Paradigms

Future Active Indicative – λύσω ("I will loose")

Person Singular Plural
1st λύσω λύσομεν
2nd λύσεις λύσετε
3rd λύσει λύσουσι(ν)

Future Middle Indicative – λύσομαι ("I will loose for myself")

Person Singular Plural
1st λύσομαι λυσόμεθα
2nd λύσῃ λύσεσθε
3rd λύσεται λύσονται

Conceptual Categories

  • σ Marker: The hallmark of the future tense is the insertion of the σ (sigma) between the present stem and the personal endings. This σ serves as the morphological indicator of futurity. For example, in λύω (“I loose”) the future becomes λύσω (“I will loose”), where λυ- + σ + ω = λύσω.
  • Voice and Meaning Differences:
    • The active future describes what the subject will do: λύσω – "I will loose."
    • The middle future often implies the subject is acting with reference to self or for personal interest: λύσομαι – "I will loose for myself."
    • Note: The future passive has distinct forms (e.g., λυθήσομαι) and is treated separately.
  • Time vs. Aspect: The future indicative primarily communicates future time, unlike other Greek tenses which emphasize aspect. The future does not inherently express either perfective or imperfective aspect; it is generally regarded as aspectually neutral or undefined. This allows the tense to focus strictly on indicating that the action will occur.
