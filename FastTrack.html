Fast Track

Alphabet

The Alphabet is the essential starting point for learning the language.

Particles

Particles can indicate logical relationships, emphasis, and speaker attitude within sentences.

Nouns Introduction

First Declension and Second Declension nouns each have two paradigms to learn.

Verbs Introduction

Indicative is the first mood to learn. There are 6 tense forms with 3 voices each.

Prepositions

Prepositions can take 1, 2, or 3 different cases as objects which can affect meaning.

Pronouns Introduction

The Basic Pronouns to learn are personal and relative pronouns.

Nouns Conclusion

Third Declension nouns are the last ones to learn.

Adjectives and Adverbs

Adjectives agree in number, case, and gender with the nouns they modify. Adverbs modify verbs and do not decline.

Participles

Participles are verbal adjectives. The forms are based on gender, case, and number as well as present, aorist, and perfect tense forms.

Pronouns Conclusion

The Other Pronouns are demonstrative, reflexive, reciprocal, interrogative, and indefinite pronouns.

Infinitives

Infinitives are verbal nouns. The forms are based on gender, case, and number as well as present, aorist, and perfect tense forms.

Verbs Conclusion

Subjuntive, Imperative, and Optative moods are the final verbs to look at.
