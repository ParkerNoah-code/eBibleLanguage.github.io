εἰμί Overview

Lexical meaning: “I am” — the basic verb of existence and identity.

Function in sentences:

  • Linking verb (copula): joins subject with predicate nominative or predicate adjective.
    Example: ὁ θεὸς φῶς ἐστιν = “God is light.”
  • Predicate adjective/noun use: μακάριοι εἰσιν = “They are blessed.”

Unlike most Greek verbs, εἰμί is irregular:

  • It has different endings than regular thematic verbs.
  • Must be memorized separately.

Basic εἰμί Paradigms

Present Indicative

Person Form Translation
1st sg. εἰμί I am
2nd sg. εἶ you are
3rd sg. ἐστί(ν) he/she/it is
1st pl. ἐσμέν we are
2nd pl. ἐστέ y'all are
3rd pl. εἰσί(ν) they are

Imperfect Indicative

Person Form Translation
1st sg. ἦν I was
2nd sg. ἦς you were
3rd sg. ἦν he/she/it was
1st pl. ἦμεν we were
2nd pl. ἦτε y'all were
3rd pl. ἦσαν they were

Future Indicative

Person Form Translation
1st sg. ἔσομαι I will be
2nd sg. ἔσῃ you will be
3rd sg. ἔσται he/she/it will be
1st pl. ἐσόμεθα we will be
2nd pl. ἔσεσθε y'all will be
3rd pl. ἔσονται they will be
