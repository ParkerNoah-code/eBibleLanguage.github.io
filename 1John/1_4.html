1 John 1:4

4 καὶ ταῦτα γράφομεν ἡμεῖς ἵνα ἡ χαρὰ ἡμῶν ᾖ πεπληρωμένη.

καὶ “and,” “also,” “even” which connects the current clause with what precedes. ταῦτα from οὗτος is the demonstrative pronoun, accusative, neuter, plural functioning as the direct object of γράφoμεν. γράφoμεν is a present, active, indicative, first person, plural verb.

ἵνα is a subordinating conjunction that introduces a purpose/result clause and governs the subjunctive mood. ἡ χαρὰ is a nominative, singular, feminie noun. Functions as the subject of the subjunctive verb clause (ᾖ πεπληρωμένη). ἡμῶν from ἐγώ, plural, possessive genitive modifying ἡ χαρὰ. ᾖ from εἰμί, present, subjunctive, third person, singular verb. Often used with ἵνα to indicate purpose/result.

πεπληρωμένη from πληρόω, perfect passive participle, feminine, singular, nominative. Functions as a predicate adjective with ᾖ. The perfect tense indicates completed action with ongoing result — fullness that has occurred and endures.
