Lessons > 1 John > Christ Our Advocate

1 John 2:1–2

1 Τεκνία μου, ταῦτα γράφω ὑμῖν ἵνα μὴ ἁμάρτητε. καὶ ἐάν τις ἁμάρτῃ, παράκλητον ἔχομεν πρὸς τὸν πατέρα Ἰησοῦν Χριστὸν δίκαιον, 2 καὶ αὐτὸς ἱλασμός ἐστιν περὶ τῶν ἁμαρτιῶν ἡμῶν, οὐ περὶ τῶν ἡμετέρων δὲ μόνον ἀλλὰ καὶ περὶ ὅλου τοῦ κόσμου.

Greek Word Parsing / Gloss
Τεκνία Vocative plural neuter of τέκνον (diminutive) – “little children”
μου Genitive singular of 1st person pronoun – “of me”, “my”
ταῦτα Demonstrative pronoun, accusative plural neuter – “these things”
γράφω Verb, 1st person singular present indicative active of γράφω – “I write”
ὑμῖν Dative plural of 2nd person pronoun – “to you”
ἵνα Conjunction – “in order that”, “so that”
μὴ Negative particle – “not” (used with non-indicative moods)
ἁμάρτητε Verb, 2nd person plural aorist subjunctive active of ἁμαρτάνω – “you might sin”
καὶ Conjunction – “and”
ἐάν Conditional particle – “if” (with subjunctive)
τις Indefinite pronoun, nominative singular – “anyone”, “someone”
ἁμάρτῃ Verb, 3rd person singular aorist subjunctive active of ἁμαρτάνω – “should sin”
παράκλητον Accusative singular masculine of παράκλητος – “advocate”, “helper”, “intercessor”
ἔχομεν Verb, 1st person plural present indicative active of ἔχω – “we have”
πρὸς Preposition with accusative – “with”, “toward”
τὸν πατέρα Accusative singular masculine noun with article – “the Father”
Ἰησοῦν Χριστὸν Proper name in accusative singular – “Jesus Christ” (object of ἔχομεν)
δίκαιον Adjective, accusative singular masculine – “righteous”
Greek Word Parsing / Gloss
καὶ Conjunction – “and”
αὐτὸς Pronoun, nominative singular masculine – “he himself” (emphatic)
ἱλασμός Noun, nominative singular masculine – “propitiation”, “atoning sacrifice”
ἐστιν Verb, 3rd person singular present indicative active of εἰμί – “is”
περὶ Preposition with genitive – “concerning”, “for”
τῶν ἁμαρτιῶν Genitive plural feminine of ἁμαρτία – “sins”
ἡμῶν Genitive plural of 1st person pronoun – “our”
οὐ Negative particle – “not”
περὶ Preposition with genitive – “for”, “concerning” (repeated for emphasis)
τῶν ἡμετέρων Genitive plural of ἡμέτερος (possessive) – “our (own)”
δὲ Conjunction – “but”, “and moreover” (mild contrastive)
μόνον Adverb – “only”, “merely”
ἀλλὰ Adversative conjunction – “but rather”
καὶ Conjunction – “also”
περὶ ὅλου Preposition + adjective, genitive singular neuter – “for the whole”
τοῦ κόσμου Noun, genitive singular masculine with article – “of the world”
