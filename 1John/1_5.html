Lessons > 1 John > God is Light

1 John 1:5

5 Καὶ ἔστιν αὕτη ἡ ἀγγελία ἣν ἀκηκόαμεν ἀπ’ αὐτοῦ καὶ ἀναγγέλλομεν ὑμῖν, ὅτι ὁ θεὸς φῶς ἐστιν καὶ σκοτία ἐν αὐτῷ οὐκ ἔστιν οὐδεμία.

Greek Word Parsing / Gloss
Καὶ Conjunction – “and”
ἔστιν Verb, 3rd person singular, present indicative active of εἰμί – “he/she/it is”
αὕτη Demonstrative pronoun, feminine nominative singular – “this”
ἡ ἀγγελία Noun, feminine nominative singular + article – “the message” or “announcement”
ἣν Relative pronoun, feminine accusative singular – “which”
ἀκηκόαμεν Verb, 1st person plural, perfect indicative active of ἀκούω – “we have heard”
ἀπ’ αὐτοῦ Preposition ἀπό + genitive pronoun – “from him”
καὶ Conjunction – “and”
ἀναγγέλλομεν Verb, 1st person plural, present indicative active of ἀναγγέλλω – “we announce” or “we proclaim”
ὑμῖν Pronoun, 2nd person plural, dative – “to you”
ὅτι Conjunction – “that” (introducing reported content)
ὁ θεὸς Noun with article, masculine nominative singular – “God”
φῶς Noun, neuter nominative singular – “light”
ἐστιν Verb, 3rd person singular, present indicative active – “is”
καὶ Conjunction – “and”
σκοτία Noun, feminine nominative singular – “darkness”
ἐν αὐτῷ Prepositional phrase – “in him”
οὐκ Negative particle used before consonants – “not”
ἔστιν Verb, 3rd person singular, present indicative active – “is”
οὐδεμία Adjective (used substantively), feminine nominative singular – “none at all” or “not even one”
